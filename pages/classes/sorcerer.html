Summary

The Sorcerer is a long-ranged class that uses a wand and robe. Their ability is the scepter, which zaps and hits multiple enemies that are close together.

Showcase of the Sorcerer's scepter


Class Unlock Requirements:

Necromancer Level 20
Assassin Level 20

Maximum Stat Cap (8/8):

HP 670
MP 385
ATK 70
DEF 25
SPD 60
DEX 60
VIT 75
WIS 60


Roles

In both solo and groups, the Sorcerer excels at clearing groups of enemies with their scepter. Clearing minions and weakening bosses for others, the Sorcerer is invaluable at dealing sustained damage to groups of enemies at a time.

If you are playing solo, your priority when it comes to dungeons is to clear rooms on your way to the boss, as that is what the class is best at doing. While you can still rush as a Sorcerer, your low defense and health stats can make mistakes almost fatal, and as such it's much safer to clear out rooms one by one on the way to the boss.

Pros

  • High-damage and effective crowd-clearing with the piercing wands and scepter ability
  • High base VIT stat to reduce timer of Vital Combat

Cons

  • Only two UT scepters that inflicts debuffs
  • Reliant on UTs, especially for damage-specific builds

Equipment Recommendations




Early Game:

Any tiered equipment you find is good enough.

If you struggle to find equipment, then the following below are easy-to-get equipment that can help suffice until you obtain better equipment

Sprite Wand

  • - Very wavy shot pattern
  • - Does not pierce enemies
  • - Very inconsistent per-shot damage
  • - Outclassed by higher tiered and most UT wands

  • + 1 extra tile of range
  • - Does not pierce enemies
  • - Low DPS, comparable to T8 Wand
 Manor of the Immortals
Honey Scepter
  • + No damage falloff on subsequent targets
  • + High amount of targets hit
  • + Low mana cost
  • - Low base damage compared to other UT scepters
  • - Low WIS scaling compared to other UT scepters

Mid Game:

Starting from this stage, you will now have a wide variety of equipment accessible to you. If you can't get any of them, it is fine to continue using tiered equipment.
