Pets Guide

Pets are very important for helping you survive. As a new player, one of your main goals should be getting a pet with the right abilities. Everything pet-related is done at the Pet Yard. You can access the Pet Yard portals in the Nexus or your Vault.

Pet Rarity

Pets have 5 levels of rarity:

  • Common (Max Ability Level Cap: 30)
  • Uncommon (Max Ability Level Cap: 50)
  • Rare (Max Ability Level Cap: 70)
  • Legendary (Max Ability Level Cap: 90)
  • Divine (Max Ability Level Cap: 100)

At Uncommon, you unlock the second ability. At Legendary, you unlock the third ability.

Pet Eggs

Hatching pet eggs in the Pet Yard is how you obtain pets. The most common way for them to drop is from Gods in the Godlands of the Realm, Dungeon Bosses, and Event Bosses. Pet Eggs drop in the form of a unique loot bag, resembling an egg basket.

Hatching Rare/Legendary pet eggs will have the second and third ability levels be randomized.

Pet Abilities

The two most important abilities to have on any pet is Heal and Magic Heal. The other abilities don't need to be as prioritized.

Optimal Ability Combination



1. Heal
2. Magic Heal
3. Electric

OR
1. Magic Heal
2. Heal
3. Electric

The 'perfect' combination that most people prefer is Heal/Magic Heal/Electric, or Magic Heal/Heal/Electric.
You don't need to worry about getting Electric as a third ability until you are close to getting a Legendary pet (Having a Rare pet with H/MH or MH/H)

I do not have a Pet with Heal and Magic Heal

If you do not have a pet with both abilities, then use a pet that has at least Heal or Magic Heal as the first ability and use it as your main pet for now until you hatch a pet with both as the first two abilities.

Feeding Pets

Feeding a pet costs Fame or Gold. The amount it costs depends on the rarity of the pet. Items have a Feed Power value which determines how much it will level up the pet.

It is important to find a good ratio of Feed Power to Fame to feed your pet. You will run out of Fame quickly by feeding too many low Feed Power items to your pet.

Only feed spare UTs you have. For example, it would be better to keep one Doom Bow instead of feeding it to use on your Archer or Huntress.

Recommended FP values for Pet Rarities:

  • Common (200-300+)
  • Uncommon (300-500+)
  • Rare (500-650+)
  • Legendary (700-900+)

For a list of items and their feed power values, check out this realmeye page here.

Fusing Pets

Fusing pets is the mechanic used to evolve a selected pet to the next rarity, while the fodder pet gets deleted in the process.

How Fusing Pets Work

Fusing pets is pretty simple, but there are some things you need to know:

  • Only fuse pets when their first ability is maxed
  • The first pet selected will be kept after fusion with their abilities, the second pet selected will be lost
  • Pet levels average out after fusion
  • You can only fuse pets with the same rarity
  • You can only fuse pets with the same family (This is not a problem as you can switch pet families)
  • You can only fuse pets if your pet yard is one rarity higher (Ex: You need an Uncommon Pet Yard to fuse 2 Common Pets)

How to Fuse Pets

Go to the Pet Yard and go to where the Pet Yard Caretaker NPC is. Two buttons at the bottom right should show up. Click on Pets and a UI window should appear.
